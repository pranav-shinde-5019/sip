What is SIP?

A Systematic Investment Plan (SIP) is an investment strategy offered by mutual funds, allowing investors to invest a fixed amount regularly (monthly, quarterly, etc.) instead of making a lump-sum investment.

SIPs help in rupee cost averaging and benefit from the power of compounding over the long term.

Benefits of SIP
  • Disciplined approach to investing
  • Rupee cost averaging reduces market timing risk
  • Power of compounding works in your favor
  • Flexibility to start with small amounts
  • Easy to automate investments
How SIP Calculator Works

The SIP calculator uses the following formula to estimate returns:

M = P × [(1 + i)^n - 1] × (1 + i)/i

Where:

  • M = Maturity amount
  • P = Periodic investment amount
  • i = Periodic interest rate (annual rate ÷ frequency)
  • n = Total number of payments (years × frequency)
Investment Tips
Past performance is not indicative of future returns. These are projected values.
  • Start early to benefit from compounding
  • Increase SIP amount periodically with income growth
  • Stay invested for long term (5+ years)
  • Diversify across asset classes
  • Review portfolio annually
